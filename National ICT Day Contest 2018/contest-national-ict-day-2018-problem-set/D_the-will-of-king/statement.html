National ICT Day Contest 2018

The Will of King

Limits: 1s, 128 MB

King Hablu wants to divide his irregular hexagonal kingdom. He will divide his land among his 3 sons, 3 daughters and his wife. Hablu’s plan is to divide the land as per the following given picture -

Hablu’s 3 sons will get the portion denoted by A, B, and C. Hablu’s 3 daughters will get the portion denoted by D, E, and F. Hablu’s wife will have the portion denoted by G. The problem is Hablu does not know the whole area of the land. He just knows what amount of portion all three sons get that is the area of A, B, C.
You are one of the best mathematicians of the Hablu’s kingdom. Hablu wants you to arrange a fair way to solve this.

Input

Input starts with an integer T denoting the number of test cases. The next T lines contain 3 integers denoting the area of A, B, C respectively. It is guaranteed that A, B, and C will always be a square number.

Constraints

1<= T <= 1000
1<= A, B, C <= 100000000

Output

For each of the T, lines print the area of the complete land that is the sum of the portion of all 3 sons, 3 daughters and his wife. Errors less than 10-4 will be ignored. Use the value of π = acos(-1) if needed.

Samples

Input Output 
1
25 16 16

88.2249899920
